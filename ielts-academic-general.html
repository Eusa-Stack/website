IELTS Academic or General Training?

Both tests assess your English ability — but they serve different purposes. Find out which test is right for your goals.

4 modules · Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking — same Speaking & Listening for both

🎓
IELTS Academic
For university & professional registration
University Route

Designed for people applying to higher education or professional registration in an English-speaking environment.

  • ✓ Applying to a university or higher education institution
  • ✓ Seeking professional registration (doctors, nurses, engineers)
  • ✓ Moving to a country for academic study
  • ✓ Enrolling in postgraduate or PhD programmes
Test Modules
Listening
30 min
Reading
60 min · Academic texts
Writing
60 min · Data interpretation
Speaking
11–14 min
Best for
Students, academics, and professionals seeking certification
🏢
IELTS General Training
For migration, work, and training programmes
Work & Migration

Reflects workplace and social survival English. Less academic content, more practical everyday reading and writing.

  • ✓ Immigration to Australia, Canada, UK, New Zealand
  • ✓ Applying for a work permit or visa
  • ✓ Completing a non-academic training programme
  • ✓ Secondary school exchange or vocational study
Test Modules
Listening
30 min
Reading
60 min · Everyday materials
Writing
60 min · Letter/report style
Speaking
11–14 min
Best for
Migrants, workers, and those on vocational training pathways
📌 Key Differences at a Glance
Reading
Academic: 3 long texts from journals, textbooks, newspapers
General: Notices, ads, manuals, workplace documents
Writing Task 1
Academic: Describe a graph, chart, table, or diagram
General: Write a formal or informal letter
Writing Task 2
Both: Essay writing — same task for Academic & General Training
