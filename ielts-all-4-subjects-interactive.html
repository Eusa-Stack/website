🎧 IELTS Listening
30 minutes · 40 questions · 4 sections (conversation → academic monologue)

📖 IELTS Reading
60 minutes · 40 questions · Academic (3 passages) or General Training (5 shorter texts)
⏱️
20 Minutes per Passage
60 minutes for 3 texts = 20 min each. Don't get stuck on one question.
🔍
Skim First, Then Scan
Read the passage heading and first sentence — get the gist. Then find keywords in questions.
📝
Answer in Order
Questions follow the order of information in the text. If you can't find it, move on.
✂️
TFNG / Yes/No/Not Given
True/False/Not Given — don't add your own knowledge. Only what the text says counts.
📄 Sample Reading Passage (Academic)

As AI systems become more sophisticated, the question of machine consciousness has moved from science fiction to serious academic debate...

✍️ IELTS Writing
60 minutes · Task 1 (150w) + Task 2 (250w) · Academic or General Training
Band 5 Underdeveloped argument
"I think social media is good because many people use it."
Click to see why →
Problem: No explanation, no examples, no development. The examiner cannot see your ability to organise ideas or support arguments.

Fix: Add a specific example and consequence: "I think social media is beneficial because platforms like LinkedIn have helped millions find employment, particularly during the pandemic when traditional job searching became impossible."
Band 6 Decent but generic
"Social media has both advantages and disadvantages. On the one hand, it allows people to connect. On the other hand, it can cause addiction."
Click to see feedback →
What's good: Balanced view, clear structure, some linking phrases.

What's missing: No specific examples, no depth to either side. Band 6 needs to show you can develop ideas, not just mention them.

Fix: "Social media has transformed how we communicate, with platforms such as Instagram enabling artists to reach global audiences. However, research from Oxford University found that heavy usage is linked to increased anxiety among teenagers aged 13–18."
Band 8 Well-developed, specific, cohesive
"The rise of remote work, accelerated by the COVID-19 pandemic, illustrates how technology reshapes professional life. A 2023 Harvard Business Review study found that 78% of knowledge workers reported higher productivity when given flexible working arrangements..."
Click to see feedback →
What makes it Band 8: Specific reference to COVID-19 context, named credible source (HBR), statistics (78%), clear cause-and-effect relationship, professional vocabulary (knowledge workers, flexible working arrangements).

Key take-away: Band 7+ requires specific examples, credible sources, and clear relevance to the question.

🗣️ IELTS Speaking
11–14 minutes · 3 parts · Face-to-face with examiner
⏱️
Part 1: Familiar Topics
2–3 min. Questions about home, family, work, hobbies. Keep answers 3–4 sentences.
🎤
Part 2: Long Turn
3–4 min. Speak for 2 minutes on a cue card topic. Use all 4 bullet points.
💬
Part 3: Discussion
4–5 min. Abstract, opinion-based questions. Extend your answers with reasons and examples.
🔗
Use Linking Phrases
Furthermore, however, in my opinion, for example — these show organisation.
🔄
Paraphrase Naturally
Don't repeat the examiner's words. Show vocabulary range by rephrasing.
😌
Stay Calm
Hesitation is natural. Use fillers like "Well, that's an interesting question..."

Record a 1-minute answer about "your hometown" and play it back to self-assess.
